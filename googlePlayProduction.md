# Google Play Console - Closed Test Production Access Answers

**Question 1: How did you recruit users for your closed test?**
I reached out to my professional network, former colleagues, and some close friends who fit our target audience. I personally messaged them explaining the app's purpose and asked if they could spare some time to test it out and give honest thoughts.

**Question 2: How easy was it to recruit testers for your app?**
Easy

**Question 3: Describe the engagement you received from testers during your closed test.**
Most testers explored the core functionalities as expected. They navigated the main sections exactly like a typical user would. A few people missed some secondary settings initially, but overall engagement was solid and closely mirrored real-world usage patterns we anticipate.

**Question 4: Provide a summary of the feedback that you received from testers. Include how you collected the feedback.**
I collected feedback mostly through direct WhatsApp messages and a short Google Form. People generally liked the clean interface and found it responsive. A couple of testers pointed out a minor navigation glitch which we fixed. Overall, they felt the app was intuitive and did what it promised.

**Question 5: Who is the intended audience of your app?**
The primary audience includes small business owners, startup founders, and independent professionals who need custom digital solutions. It is designed for anyone looking to establish or improve their online presence through tailored web development and technical consulting services.

**Question 6: Describe how your app provides value to users.**
It offers a streamlined way for clients to discover our services, view past work portfolios, and directly request project consultations. This saves them time compared to traditional email inquiries and provides clear upfront information about our development capabilities and processes.

**Question 7: How many installs do you expect your app to have in your first year?**
0 - 10K

**Question 8: What changes did you make to your app based on what you learned during your closed test?**
We fixed a minor navigation bug where the back button didn't work correctly on one of the service pages. We also improved the loading speed for the portfolio images based on comments that they were taking too long to appear on slower network connections. Everything else worked smoothly.

**Question 9: How did you decide that your app is ready for production?**
The testers used the app for several days without experiencing any crashes or major issues. Since we successfully addressed the small pieces of feedback they provided, and the core features are performing reliably, we feel confident putting the app into the hands of real users.
